Tottenham is a club from North London founded in 1882. They were founded by 11 schoolboys and slowly gained more and more popularity as the years went on. They did not become a professional team until 1895. Tottenham played in the stadium White Hart Lane until 2015 when the commencement of Tottenham Hotspurs Stadium began. The stadium was finished in 2019 and is one of the largest, most industrial stadiums in the world. Today Tottenham are a wealthy well businessed club, who're highly regarded as a world class team within the top fifteen teams in the world. They're also one of the founding teams in the Premier League where it started in 1992 and still going on today. The Premier League is the most popular and highest level soccer league in the world and Tottenham shares the platform with many other powerhouse teams such as Manchester United, Manchester City, Liverpool, Nottingham Forest, Newcastle United, Aston Villa, etc.

Tottenham has always been a very successful team during their time as a professional club. They have won countless trophies, despite the jokes due to the 17 year trophy draught. Even in the trophy draught they have been close to winning many trophies and still had many world class players. Some trophies they came close to winning were the 2016-2017 Premier League Champions, the 2019 UEFA Champions, 2021 EFL Final. After all these attempts to win a trophy the club got the adjective spursy, which means they choke at the last minute or get so close and then fail. The trophy draught ended this year (2025) in the Europa League Final vs Manchester United. Tottenham won 1-0 and the winner was scored by the Welshman Brennan Johnson.

Derbies in soccer are like rivalries in other sports, but unlike a NY Yankees vs Boston Redsox rivalry it is by location. In the English Premier League we have derbies between teams from the same areas. In London, we have the North London between Tottenham & Arsenal, as well as the West London derby between Chelsea, Brentford, Fulham and QPR. These games are huge in magnitude, the meaning and passion go far beyond a regular game. The North London Derby is a long lasting rivalry, the first one being in 1921. For the players and fans it means everything, it is an opportunity for bragging rights and 3 points.
